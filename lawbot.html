Labor LawBot

AI-Powered Labor Law Precedent Assistant

Overview

Labor LawBot is an AI-powered chatbot designed to provide workers and practitioners with precise and reliable information based on labor law precedents. Using a dataset of 1,089 labor law cases spanning from 2010 to 2023, the system offers case-specific insights to streamline workplace conflict resolution and reduce emotional distress.

Impact: Labor LawBot ensures accessibility to legal knowledge for individuals with little or no legal background while aiding professionals by simplifying complex case searches and interpretations.

Project Scope

This project involved designing and developing a chatbot’s legal knowledge base and user experience, including:

  • Data Collection and Analysis
  • Azure AI Search Indexing and Semantic Layer Configuration
  • Integration with OpenAI GPT and System Parameter Optimization
  • Gradio Web App Design and Implementation

Target Users

Employees looking for easy access to legal information, HR professionals managing workplace compliance.

Interface

Web App

Timeline

1.5 weeks

Problems Solving

Problem 1

Labor law information is often inaccessible or difficult to interpret for non-experts.

💡 Solution: Labor LawBot utilizes a conversational AI model enhanced with indexed labor law precedents, providing simplified and case-specific insights to users.

Problem 2

Generative AI models occasionally produce hallucinated information, which can be problematic in a precedent-based service.

💡 Solution: To mitigate hallucinations, the model’s response content was strictly limited to the indexed dataset by maximizing data resource strictness and setting a low temperature to reduce creative responses. Additionally, cited data references were included in answers, enabling users to directly verify the source of information.

Problem 3

Providing up-to-date, accurate, and trustworthy legal information is a challenge.

💡 Solution: A curated dataset of 1,089 labor law precedents ensures reliable insights. The system directs users to trusted legal databases like LawInfo for further detailed searches.

Process

Labor LawBot was developed through a structured process to ensure accuracy and usability, leveraging state-of-the-art AI and data management tools.

1. Data Collection

Gathered 1,089 labor law cases from 2010 to 2023, focusing on diverse workplace challenges. The data was cleaned, formatted, and verified for reliability. Metadata such as case numbers, rulings, and summaries were added for structured indexing.

2. Indexing & Semantic Search

Utilized Azure AI Search to index labor law cases. A semantic layer was added to improve natural language query handling, allowing users to search using everyday language while retrieving precise case references.

3. GPT Model Integration

Integrated OpenAI GPT to generate detailed and context-aware responses. Adjusted system parameters to ensure the chatbot aligns its answers with indexed cases, maintaining accuracy and trustworthiness.

4. Web Application Development

Developed a user-friendly web application using Gradio, allowing users to interact with the chatbot seamlessly. The interface includes simple input fields for queries and options for detailed follow-up searches.

5. Result

Developed a clean and user-friendly web app. Users can ask questions about labor laws, and the LawBot searches the database to provide accurate answers. Each response includes related case numbers at the bottom, allowing users to copy and continue their inquiries seamlessly.

Question: If I've worked for more than one year but less than two years, how many vacation days am I entitled to?

Answer: you have worked for a period of more than one year but less than two years, the number of annual leave days (annual paid vacation) is as follows.

If you've worked 80% or more of the year, you're entitled to 15 days of annual leave. If you have worked for less than a year, your annual leave is calculated proportionally to the number of days you have worked. So, if you've worked for more than one year but less than two years, you'll be granted 15 days of annual leave, and you may accrue additional days based on your attendance rate during this time [doc1]. Furthermore, the use of annual leave is regulated by the Labor Standards Act, and unused annual leave may be carried forward .

Relevant case numbers: 2016Da48297, 2021Da227100.
